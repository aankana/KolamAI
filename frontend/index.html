What is Kolam ?

Kolam is a centuries-old floor-art tradition in Tamil Nadu and neighboring regions, drawn every morning on thresholds using rice flour. Women learn Kolam matrilineally from childhood, and well-composed kolams (sikku Kolam) are socially valued as signs of patience and skill.

UNESCO notes that Kolam patterns are explicitly "symmetrical" and "based on geometry and mathematical principles". Indeed, researchers observe that Kolam consists of closed loops weaving around a a regular grid of dots ("pulli"), following implicit rules. In sum, Kolam is both an integral cultural symbol and a naturally geometric art form exhibiting symmetry, repetition and other mathematical ideas.
